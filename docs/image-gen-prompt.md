# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

These prompts depict the envisioned sheng-style free-reed mouth organ described in
`design.md` and the visual/drawing briefs. They are concept visualizations and must
not set pipe length, reed-window size, socket centers, or windchest geometry. The
design carries two architecture branches (a traditional long-pipe side-branch sheng
with both pipe ends open, and a compact stopped-end control module); the hero and
in-context views show the recognizable bundled-pipe mouth-organ silhouette without
committing to measured pipe lengths or reed dimensions.

## Hero Prompt

A finished concept render of a sheng-style Chinese free-reed mouth organ standing
upright as a single hand-held instrument: a tight circular bundle of slender vertical
bamboo-toned pipes of varied lengths rising from a rounded cup-shaped windchest at the
base, with a short curved blow/draw mouthpiece tube projecting from the side of the
windchest. The pipes are gathered close like a sheaf, each a smooth turned tube with a
small fingering hole near its lower end where it enters the chest; the windchest reads
as a sealed metal-and-wood cup that bundles the pipe feet together. Materials and finish
implied by the design: warm honey-toned bamboo or pale hardwood pipes, a darker
lacquered or brushed-metal windchest cup, subtle visible reed sockets where pipes meet
the chest. Realistic hand-scale cues (an instrument about the height of a forearm),
clean neutral light-gray studio background, soft directional studio light from upper
left, 3/4 product-photography angle, ~50mm lens, shallow falloff, high detail,
photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

The same bundled-pipe sheng concept staged on a maker's workbench in a quiet instrument
workshop: held lightly upright in two hands as if about to be played, the curved
mouthpiece near the player's lips, soft window daylight raking across the pipe bundle.
Background shows out-of-focus shop storytelling — a tuning bench, hand tools, a coil of
gasket cord, wood shavings — establishing this as a prototype free-reed mouth organ in
development. Natural light, shallow depth of field with the pipe bundle in crisp focus,
warm honest tones, concept visualization only, no text or callouts.

## Detail Prompt

A macro close-up at the base of the sheng where the slender pipes plug into the rounded
windchest cup: the signature free-reed sockets and pipe feet, showing how each pipe
seats into its gasketed socket with a small free reed implied at the closed boundary,
and a single fingering hole on the nearest pipe wall. Emphasize materials and textures —
fine bamboo grain, the seam where pipe meets the lacquered chest, a hint of brass reed
plate in shadow — under raking sidelight that brings out turning marks and the gasket
land. High detail, photorealistic, shallow depth of field, no measurements, no callouts.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension lines, hole-spacing
callouts, tuning numbers, or fabrication specifications; no impossible mechanisms or
free reeds drawn as fantasy parts; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
